# FounderNexus Comparison Page Playbook

Read by: human writer, page reviewer, future article generator
Depends on: `campaign-lanes.md`, `routing-rules.md`, `content-standards.md`

## Why this exists

Comparison pages are a content type, not a campaign lane.

A page such as `YPO alternatives for founders` is not automatically a Founder Nomination asset. It may attract several reader types:

- a founder evaluating peer options for themselves
- a member, VC, advisor, or operator thinking of a founder to refer
- a not-ready founder who should stay in the relationship reservoir

The page should explain the FounderNexus distinction and then route the reader according to intent.

## Default lane

Default lane: `linkedin-assisted-demand`

Reason: comparison pages clarify the worldview, capture search and social demand, and warm qualified founders before a specific conversion moment exists.

Allowed alternate lanes:

- `founder-nomination` only when the page is written explicitly for members or trusted sources.
- `vc-fast-pass` only when the comparison is about fundraising access or portfolio support.
- `event-conversion` only when the page is tied to a specific session.

## Page job

A comparison page should answer one question:

Which founder decision environment is right for the reader right now?

It should not:

- attack named competitors
- claim FounderNexus is better for everyone
- turn the page into generic SEO content
- force a nomination CTA when the reader is clearly evaluating for themselves

## CTA routing model

Use one primary CTA on the page. Use secondary links only when they are clearly lower-friction and do not compete with the primary ask.

Default primary CTA:

- `Explore whether FounderNexus fits your stage`

Default secondary paths:

- `Nominate a founder` for members, VCs, advisors, and trusted sources
- `Stay close` for not-ready founders or readers without a live decision

Do not publish any CTA route until the destination exists and has been checked live.

## Recommended page structure

1. Lead with the decision standard, not the competitor.
2. Explain the difference between access, community, and stage-specific decision rooms.
3. Name who each option is likely best for, respectfully.
4. Explain where FounderNexus fits.
5. Give the reader a clear next step based on their role.

## Review gates

- Named competitor language needs Court and legal review.
- Any claim about another organization must be factual, sourced, and neutral.
- Any FounderNexus claim must fit the messaging framework and proof rules.
- CTA routes must return a live 200 before publication.
