# FounderNexus Engagement Guardrails

Read by: engagement brief builder, anyone actioning a brief
Applies to: LinkedIn, DMs, comment engagement, any 1:1 outreach touched by the system

## 1. Core rule

Automation prepares. Humans send. Nothing in this system posts, comments, or messages on anyone's behalf without a person reading it first and clicking send.

## 2. No scraping, no auto-engagement

Engagement briefs are built from Attio signals (stage, funding recency, engagement history, referral source), never from scraping LinkedIn activity or profiles. If a signal isn't already in Attio, it isn't in the brief.

## 3. Voice ownership

Court owns LinkedIn engagement in his own voice. Draft replies in the brief are starting points, not final copy. Nobody posts as Court except Court.

## 4. Suppression is absolute

Anyone Attio marks suppressed is excluded from every brief, every draft, every target list. No manual override without Court's explicit sign-off logged in Attio.

## 5. Brief cadence and format

Twice weekly, Monday and Thursday. Each brief lists at most 8 founders or conversations worth engaging, with: why they matter, the trust path (how we know them, how well), a draft response, and the recommended ask if any. The owner reviews, edits, sends, or discards each item.

## 6. Escalate, don't automate

These always route to a human judgment call before a brief entry is even drafted, not just before sending:

- Anything touching a competitor by name.
- Anything where the founder has expressed frustration, distress, or a sensitive personal situation.
- Anything ambiguous about whether the person wants to be engaged with at all.

## 7. Tone check

FounderNexus is a peer environment, not a sales funnel. Every draft reply should read like a peer noticing something worth a real reply, not a rep working a sequence. If a draft could be mistaken for outreach automation, rewrite it or drop it.
