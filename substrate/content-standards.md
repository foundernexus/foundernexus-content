# FounderNexus Content Standards

Read by: article generator, review-pass script, human reviewer
Applies to: all articles, LinkedIn posts, email blurbs, newsletter copy

## 1. Positioning anchor

FounderNexus is judgment infrastructure for venture-scale founders, Stage 2 to 3, $2 to 50M ARR. Every piece of content earns its place by helping a founder make a better decision, not by ranking for a keyword. If a draft cannot name the decision it helps a founder make, it does not ship.

## 2. Required terminology

Use only current vocabulary. Legacy terms are permanently retired, even in casual reference.

| Use | Never |
|---|---|
| Stage 1 to 4 | Tier 1 to 5 |
| Nexus Partner | Chapter chair |
| Stage-based room | Chapter |
| Session | Event (except in existing URLs) |

A draft containing a retired term fails review automatically. No exceptions for "just this once."

## 3. Voice

- CEO-brief: the point comes before the setup. TL;DR or a lead sentence states the conclusion first.
- No em dashes. Use periods or commas.
- No filler ("in today's fast-paced world," "let's dive in").
- Direct claims over hedged ones. If something is uncertain, say what's uncertain rather than softening with vague qualifiers.
- Prose over bullet-stacking, except in comparisons or steps.
- Every article reads like Court or Robroy is talking to one specific founder, not a segment.

## 4. Structural requirements

- One core argument per article, stated in the first two sentences.
- Every claim about founder pain, funding, or market data needs a source. No invented statistics.
- One CTA per article, matched to its lane per `routing-rules.md`. Never CTA-stack.
- 800 to 1400 words for pillar articles. Shorter is fine. Padding to hit a word count is not.

## 5. Lane gate

No article generates without an approved lane in `campaign-lanes.md`. Keyword volume and search intent are necessary, not sufficient.

## 6. What never ships

- Paid-ads framing or PLG/freemium framing. Both are named positioning hazards for this business.
- Generic "startup advice" content indistinguishable from any other founder blog.
- Content targeting or referencing anyone Attio marks suppressed.
- Competitive callouts by name without legal and Court's sign-off.
- AI-written content that reads as AI-written. If the dual-pass review flags generic phrasing, rewrite before it reaches the human queue.

## 7. Dual-pass review logic

**Pass 1, structure:** lane assigned, terminology clean, every claim sourced, CTA matches `routing-rules.md`.
**Pass 2, voice:** reads like a specific founder conversation, no filler, no em dashes, TL;DR present.

A draft that fails either pass returns to generation with the specific failure noted, not a generic "try again."
