TL;DR: if you're fundraising right now, the question isn't "does cold outreach ever work." It's where your next ten hours of outreach effort go: writing and sending cold emails to VC associates, or working the relationships that could produce a warm introduction instead. That's a real trade-off, because both take real time, and you can't fully do both well at once.

Most founders answer this question by instinct rather than by looking at it directly. Cold outreach feels productive because you can measure it: emails sent, opens, replies. Relationship-building feels slow and unmeasurable, so it gets deprioritized exactly when time is tightest.

## The honest case for cold outreach

Cold outreach isn't a lost cause. Some VCs genuinely read cold inbound, especially at firms that have built process around it, and a founder with a thin network or a very early company sometimes has no warm path available yet. In that position, cold outreach is not a mistake. It's the only lever you have.

<div class="callout fair">
<h3>If this describes you, cold outreach is a reasonable default</h3>
<p>You're pre-seed or very early, your network doesn't yet include anyone with standing at the funds you want to reach, and you need reps at outreach and pitching regardless of conversion rate. Cold outreach done well, tightly targeted, evidence-first, still teaches you things a warm path won't.</p>
</div>

## Why a warm path usually wins once you have any relationships to work with

A deck arriving via a trusted introduction gets read differently than the same deck arriving cold. Not because the content changed. Because the investor's first read of any inbound is "do I trust the source enough to spend real time on this," and a warm intro answers that question before a single slide is opened.

<ul class="clean">
<li><strong>Credibility transfer.</strong> A trusted introducer is putting their own judgment on the line. That changes how the receiving investor weighs the first few minutes.</li>
<li><strong>Time efficiency.</strong> One warm intro to the right person can be worth more than fifty cold emails, because it starts past the trust filter instead of at it.</li>
<li><strong>Compounding.</strong> Relationships you build now for this raise are still there for the next one. Cold-email lists mostly aren't.</li>
</ul>

## How to decide where your next ten hours go

If you have zero usable relationships with people who have standing at your target funds, spend the hour on a tightly targeted cold email. There's no warm path to build yet, and reps matter.

If you have any relationships worth activating, even weak ones, the higher-leverage hour is almost always spent turning those into an actual warm introduction rather than writing one more cold email. The intro you could have earned is usually worth more than the next five cold sends combined.
