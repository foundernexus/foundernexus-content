# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

Most founders facing a hard decision are not short on feedback. They're short on feedback close enough to the problem to actually change their mind.

Ten conversations, and not one of them moved you. That's the tell. Feedback that only confirms what you already believed isn't feedback. It's company.

Good input passes three tests: recent, changes the shape of the decision, and comes from someone with no stake in your answer.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

Before your next round of calls on a hard decision, run each one through a short filter: has this person carried something close to this recently, will they tell me something that could change the call, and do they have any reason to want a particular answer from me.

If the honest answer to any of those is no, the conversation might still be worth having. It's just not the one that should move the decision.

Vague questions get vague answers. Name the decision precisely before you go looking for outside signal.
