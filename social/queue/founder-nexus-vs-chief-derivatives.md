# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

Some peer networks are organized around a level: senior executives, across every kind of company, brought together by seniority and a shared professional identity. That is real value, and for a lot of leaders it is the right room.

FounderNexus is organized around a different axis entirely: not your title, but the venture-scale decision you're carrying, with peers matched to your stage.

Seniority or the specific call. Two different questions.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

The honest way to compare two rooms is to ask what each is organized around.

A network of senior executives across functions and industries is organized around who you are, your level, your leadership identity.

FounderNexus is organized around what you're carrying, a specific venture decision, with founders at your stage. For most people, one of those two descriptions clearly fits and the other clearly doesn't. That's the whole answer.
