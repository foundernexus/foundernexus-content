# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

The question isn't does cold email work.

It's should I write this cold email tonight, or spend that hour somewhere else.

That's a mechanics question, and it has a real answer: stage-matched firm, one piece of evidence, under 150 words, the smallest possible ask, a real follow-up cadence. Miss any of those and you're not saving effort. You're spending it slower.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

Founders who conclude cold email doesn't work are often judging it against the wrong outcome, a term sheet, instead of the one it can actually produce, a first conversation.

Redefine the win, and the honest question becomes answerable: under what conditions does this reliably get a reply worth having.

## Short Post 1

A fund that only writes Series B checks getting a pre-seed cold email isn't going to reply "wrong stage, but let me help."

They're going to archive it. No amount of writing skill fixes a targeting problem.

## Short Post 2

Cold email that works is short enough to read on a phone in the time it takes to decide whether to keep reading.

Usually under 150 words. Length reads as a request for the reader's time before you've earned it.

## Newsletter Blurb

New on the blog: does cold email to VCs ever work? Yes, under specific, nameable conditions, and no, most of the time it doesn't reliably work when founders skip one of them. The honest breakdown of when it's worth the hour and when it's a slower channel that just feels like progress.
