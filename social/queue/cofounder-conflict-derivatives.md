# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

Most co-founder conflict gets diagnosed wrong, in both directions.

Friction is a fight about how you run the company: roles, communication, who owns which call. A split is a fight about what company you're running at all.

The first is fixable and gets treated like the end. The second is fundamental and gets papered over as a rough patch. Naming which one you're actually in is the whole job.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

The question founders ask first is "can we keep working together." It's the wrong one to start with.

The useful question is narrower: is the thing you disagree about fixable inside the partnership, or is it a disagreement about the partnership itself?

Better meetings fix the first. Nothing fixes the second except an honest conversation about the destination.

## Short Post 1

Friction is a fight about how you run the company.

A split is a fight about what company you're running.

Treating one like the other is how good partnerships end badly and bad ones limp on too long.

## Short Post 2

You and your co-founder disagreeing is not the signal.

Whether you still grant each other good faith is. That's the line between a rough stretch and a real split.
