# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

Most founder communities sell access. Bigger network, more chapters, more names you'd recognize.

Access is not the same as relevance.

When a decision is expensive, the founder who helps you most is not the one with the biggest name. It's the one who carried something close to your exact call, recently, at your stage.

That's a different filter than "how many members."

If you're evaluating a peer environment right now, ask what it's actually organized around: broad membership, or the decision in front of you.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

We get compared to the large, well-established peer networks a lot. Fair question.

The honest answer: those networks are genuinely good at what they do, structured, confidential, built for the long haul.

FounderNexus is built narrower on purpose. Venture-scale founders only. Rooms organized by stage. Convened around the decision you're actually carrying, not a standing monthly calendar slot.

Different tool, different job.
