# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

The founder community we get compared to most is also one of the best-built ones out there. Excellent curation, a real core-group model, a genuinely strong whole-founder conversation.

So why build something narrower?

Because breadth and depth trade off. A community built to serve founders and CEOs across every kind of company can't also be the room that shares your exact board dynamics, your exact dilution math, your exact growth pressure.

FounderNexus picks depth. Venture-scale only, organized by stage, convened around the live decision.

Neither choice is wrong. They're different tools.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

Close call, and we'll say that plainly: if you want a broad, polished founder community that covers your whole life, not just the business, the well-known core-group model is genuinely excellent. It might be the better fit for you.

FounderNexus optimizes for something narrower on purpose: venture-scale founders only, rooms matched to stage, convened around the specific decision you're carrying.

Both are high-signal. They're just built for different jobs.

## Short Post 1

Whole-founder breadth or venture-decision depth.

Both are legitimate. Know which one you actually need before you pick a room.

## Short Post 2

A great peer community and the right peer room for your specific decision are not always the same thing.

Ask which one you're actually solving for.
