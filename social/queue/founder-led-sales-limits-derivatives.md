# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

Founder-led sales doesn't fail loudly. It plateaus, at a very specific number: however many deals one founder can personally carry.

The trap isn't that founder-led sales stops working. It's that it works well enough, for long enough, that you never build anything to replace it.

Stepping away feels like failure at exactly the moment you need to commit to doing it.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

The honest question underneath the first sales hire: do you have a repeatable motion, or a talented founder closing on instinct?

You can't hand a salesperson "be as compelling as the founder." You can hand them a motion.

Founders who get this right build the motion legible themselves first, then hire someone to run and refine it, not invent it.

## Short Post 1

Founder-led sales doesn't fail loudly. It plateaus.

Growth flattening to the shape of your calendar is the signal, not a coincidence.

## Short Post 2

You're hiring a salesperson to run a motion.

If the motion doesn't exist yet, you're actually asking them to build it, and that's a different hire.
