# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

One genuinely useful conversation before a big call does not just make you feel more confident.

It changes what you actually do next.

A conversation that only raises your conviction in the call you'd already made is pleasant and close to worthless. The useful one sends you out with a decision that has a different shape.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

The reason deciding alone feels cheaper than it is: the cost of a solo call that goes wrong is loud and lands months later, far from the decision. The cost of finding a good conversation is quiet and lands now, an afternoon and a bit of swallowed pride.

The brain weights the visible near cost over the invisible far one. The math usually runs the other way. One avoided bad hire pays for a lot of conversations.
