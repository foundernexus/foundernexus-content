# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

When you think about nominating someone, the real question is not whether they're a good founder.

It's whether this person makes this room better, or just bigger.

Run the founder you have in mind against the room you actually have, not the room in the abstract. A great founder two stages away from everyone else can still be the wrong fit for this table.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

Three tests for whether a founder fits a specific room:

Stage close enough that the exchange runs both ways.
Would they bring a live decision, or a highlight reel.
Would they protect the room's candor, or spend it.

A yes on all three is a nomination. A no on any of them is a founder you like who belongs in a different room.

## Short Post 1

The best founder in the world can be the wrong fit for a specific room.

Fit is stage, a live decision, and candor. Not resume.

## Short Post 2

Before you put a name forward: does this person make the room better, or just bigger?

Bigger is easy. Better is the whole point.
