# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

A peer room earns its place when a founder brings a specific live decision to it and leaves with the decision changed, not just discussed.

The mechanism is simple and easy to get wrong: name a real call you're carrying, put it in front of people who've carried something close, and let them press on the parts you've been avoiding.

If a room doesn't run that way, it's a meeting.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

The unit of value in a peer room is a live decision, not an update.

"Here's how the quarter went" produces nods. "I have eleven months of runway, a board that wants growth, and a senior hire I'm no longer sure about, and I have to decide in three weeks" produces the session that changes something.

Specificity is the tell. Vague questions get vague reactions.

## Short Post 1

Bring a decision, not an update.

A room reacting to "here's how it went" is a discussion club. A room pressure-testing a real call is a peer room.

## Short Post 2

The test of whether you'll get value from a peer room isn't only "do I have hard decisions to bring."

It's "am I willing to do the work on other people's." That's how you get sharper at your own.
