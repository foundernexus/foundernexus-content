# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

Nobody warns you about the real shift after Series B.

It isn't that the board turns against you. It's that the board becomes an actual board, a governance body, with real power, judging a company none of its members have ever run.

Founders who miss this keep treating the board as a cheering section, and they're genuinely surprised when it stops behaving like one.

The board meeting should be where decisions get ratified, not where people learn things for the first time.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

The highest-stakes board moments, a missed number, a pivot, a leadership problem, are the ones founders least want to walk into cold.

The founders who handle them well pressure-test the hard conversation before the meeting, with people who have sat on the other side of that exact table.

Do it live in the room, and you lose control of the narrative. Do it ahead of time, and you keep it.

## Short Post 1

The board doesn't turn against you after Series B.

It turns into an actual board. Managing that well is a learned skill, not an instinct.

## Short Post 2

Will anyone learn something for the first time in your next board meeting?

If the answer is yes, that's the problem to fix before the meeting, not during it.
