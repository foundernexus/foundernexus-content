# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

A bridge round is one of two things, and founders reach for it before they've been honest about which.

Either it buys time to hit one specific, nameable milestone that changes the story a real investor will fund. Or it delays the harder call about whether the business works.

Same instrument, opposite meaning. The whole decision turns on which one you're actually in.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

The honest test for a bridge: can you name the single milestone the money buys, and would hitting it change the answer from a serious investor?

If yes, a bridge is a real tool.

If the honest answer is "it buys me more time to figure that out," you're not bridging to a milestone. You're renting time, at a higher burn, to arrive at the same no.

## Short Post 1

A bridge round buys you a milestone or it buys you time.

Only one of those is worth the dilution.

## Short Post 2

Before you raise a bridge, finish this sentence: "This money gets us to ___, and hitting it changes the story because ___."

If you can't, the bridge isn't the decision. The story is.
