# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

The price you put on your first enterprise deal is an anchor you live with.

The real question is not "what will they pay." It's whether you're pricing to win this logo, or pricing to set what the product is worth for every deal that follows.

Discount hard to close the first one, and you've bought a customer and sold your pricing power in the same signature.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

There is a way to win the first logo without wrecking the anchor: make the discount explicitly about being first, not about the price being high.

A founding-customer rate. A design-partner arrangement. A number that comes with a reason it won't repeat.

That protects the worth of the product for deal number ten, which is the one that actually matters.

## Short Post 1

Your first enterprise price isn't a number. It's an anchor.

Every deal after it negotiates against the one you set now.

## Short Post 2

Winning the logo and setting the price are two different goals.

If you discount to win, make the discount about being first, not about the product being worth less.
