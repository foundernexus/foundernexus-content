# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

The first senior hire at seed looks like a staffing decision. It isn't.

It's a decision about what the company needs to become next, and most founders make it under exactly the wrong pressure: something is slipping, a senior hire feels like relief, and relief is not a hiring strategy.

The test that actually matters: can you describe what "working" looks like in this function today? If the honest answer is no, you don't need an executive yet. You need to keep building it yourself.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

The most expensive version of a first senior hire is bringing in someone to scale a function that doesn't exist yet.

They expect a machine to optimize. They find a blank page. Building from a blank page is founder work, not executive work, and that mismatch usually takes six months and one painful exit to surface.

Before the offer: is the seat real, or are you still the one who has to invent it?

## Short Post 1

A senior hire scales something that already works.

If it doesn't work yet, you're not hiring an executive. You're outsourcing a decision you still have to make yourself.

## Short Post 2

Builder, or manager in builder's clothing?

One talks about the messiest early version of what they made. The other asks about headcount before day one.

Know which one is across the table.
