# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

Fundraising founders treat outreach as a volume problem. Send more emails, meet more people, eventually something lands.

That's the wrong lever.

One warm intro to the right person is often worth more than fifty cold emails, because it starts past the trust filter instead of at it. The question isn't does cold outreach work. It's where your next ten hours actually go.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

Cold outreach isn't a mistake. If you're pre-seed with a thin network, it's sometimes the only lever you have.

But once you have any relationships worth activating, even weak ones, the higher-leverage hour is almost always spent turning those into a real introduction, not writing one more cold email.

Know which stage you're actually in before you decide where the hour goes.

## Short Post 1

A warm intro clears the trust filter before the investor opens your deck.

A cold email has to clear it alone, from zero.

That's the entire difference.

## Short Post 2

The relationships you build for this raise are still there for the next one.

Cold-email lists mostly aren't.
