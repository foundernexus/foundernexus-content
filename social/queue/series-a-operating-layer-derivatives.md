# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

The money is the visible part of a Series A. The harder part is invisible: the exact way you ran the company to get here is the way it stops working from here on.

At seed, you are the operating layer. Every decision runs through you, and that's correct, it's how a small company should run.

After Series A, building a real operating layer, systems, rhythm, decision rights that don't require you in every loop, becomes the actual job. Most founders are slow to notice the job changed at all.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

The instinct after a raise is to add: more hires, more initiatives, more surface area.

The harder, more important move is subtraction. Which decisions do you stop making yourself, so the team can actually own them?

A decision made a little worse without you is often better than a decision that only happens when you're in the room. That trade is uncomfortable. It's also the job now.

## Short Post 1

The company that got you to Series A ran on you being in every decision.

The one after it has to run on systems that let you not be.

## Short Post 2

Founder-led sales is a superpower at seed. So is founder-led everything.

After Series A, the exact thing that worked becomes the ceiling. Building the system that replaces you is the actual work now.
