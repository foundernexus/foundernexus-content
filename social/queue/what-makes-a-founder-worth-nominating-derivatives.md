# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

A good nomination is not the founder you like most or owe a favor to.

It's the founder whose presence would make the room sharper for everyone in it.

Nominating someone you like is easy and mostly harmless to you. It is not harmless to the other founders whose next hard decision depends on who is sitting at the table with them.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

Every seat in a stage-based room is load-bearing. A nomination is not an invitation to a party. It's a vote about whose pattern recognition the rest of the room will lean on when the stakes are high.

So the test is simple: would this founder raise the standard of the conversation, or just add a name to it?

Nominate on the first. Hold on the second.

## Short Post 1

The nomination test is one question: would this founder's presence make the room sharper?

Not more impressive. Not bigger. Sharper.

## Short Post 2

Nominating a founder you like is a kindness to them.

Nominating a founder who raises the room is a service to everyone already in it. Only one of those protects the thing that makes the room worth being in.
