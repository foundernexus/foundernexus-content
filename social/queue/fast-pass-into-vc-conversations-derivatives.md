# Social Derivatives

## LinkedIn, Court Voice

A fast-pass into VC conversations is not a favor a well-connected friend grants you.

It's what happens when someone with a reputation to protect stakes it on a specific claim about your company.

The founder decision underneath it isn't who do I know who can make an intro. It's what would need to be true about your story before anyone would take that risk.

## LinkedIn, FounderNexus Voice

Not everyone who likes you can fast-pass you.

The people who can are the ones whose judgment an investor already trusts, built over years, through a track record of never sending a bad one.

Founders often look for the person with the biggest network. They should be looking for the person whose forward actually gets opened.

## Short Post 1

The signal in a warm intro isn't the introduction.

It's the introducer's willingness to be judged by it.

## Short Post 2

A fast-pass buys you attention. It doesn't buy you a check.

The intro got you in the room. It didn't make the next thirty minutes any less unforgiving.
